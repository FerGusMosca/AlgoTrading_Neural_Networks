The iShares MSCI Brazil Capped ETF (NYSE: EWZ), the largest exchange traded fund dedicated to Brazilian equities, traded slightly higher in Wednesday's after-hours session after the central bank there pared the benchmark Selic rate by 50 basis points to 6%.  The $9 billion EWZ, which tracks the MSCI Brazil 25/50 Index, is up 19.35% year to date, or about double the gains of the MSCI Emerging Markets Index.
